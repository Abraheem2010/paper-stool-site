Frame by frame: how Paper Stool improves comfort and space usage

Frame 01

Crowded platform, not enough seats

In rush hour, many passengers stand because fixed seating is limited and centralized. Comfort drops fast when wait times get longer.

  • Problem: limited public seating points
  • Impact: fatigue, crowd pressure, poor waiting experience

Frame 02

The stool travels with you

Folded form is compact enough to live inside a backpack or tote. You carry comfort only when needed, with no heavy chair footprint.

Open, sit, continue

Opening takes seconds. You do not need tools, setup space, or assistance. Just unfold, place, sit, and fold back when your train arrives.

Space Logic

Why it works better in crowded environments

Distributed seating model

Fixed benches create one crowded hotspot. Portable personal stools distribute seating across small available pockets of space.

Zero permanent footprint

When not in use, the stool folds flat and disappears into a bag. No visual clutter, no blocked pathways, no heavy object storage.

Faster comfort access

Quick open and close makes it practical for short waits, not only long sits. That makes adoption easier in everyday routines.
